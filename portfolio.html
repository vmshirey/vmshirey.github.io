Research Portfolio

Climate Change +
Arctic Butterflies

Role: Project Lead
In Revision

I used occupancy-detection models to reconstruct a half-century of butterfly community dynamics in Arctic and boreal North America. I found a stark decline among this regions endemic butterfly fauna due to rising temperatures.

Bayesian Occupancy-detection Models

Role: Project Lead
Methods in Ecology + Evolution

The most abundant source of biodiversity data, species occurrences, are notoriously biased. In this work, I led a team that tested the efficacy of Bayesian occupancy-detection models for cutting through this bias to produce robust estimates of historical biodiversity change.

Climate Change + Chagas Vectors

Role: Project Lead
In Revision

How does our current knowledge of kissing bug distributions match with species distribution models? This work examines this question and asks where sampling should occur of this important group of insects under future climate change scenarios.

Global Butterfly Traits

Role: Project Lead
Scientific Data

I led an international team of researchers to create LepTraits, the world's largest dataset of butterfly trait information which covers ~12,500 species of butterfly or about 66% of all described species in the world.

Honey and Bumblebee Competition via AI

Role: Mentor
In Prep

I am currently surpervising a senior undergraduate student at Georgetown University who is leveraging deep computer vision to study potential sub-continental signals of competition between honeybees and native bumblebees using participatory science photographs.

Fire Impacts on Insects

Role: Methodology
Biological Conservation

How does fire impact insects? Turns out the answer is more complicated than you think. In this work, I created the meta-analysis pipeline to answer this question from previous studies conducted on fire and insect populations.
