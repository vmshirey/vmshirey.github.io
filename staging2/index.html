We use butterflies and moths to assess
the impact of human activity on Earth's biodiversity.

Our lab employs a variety of data science tools including artificial intelligence and Bayesian hierarchical models to understand the impact of global change and to plan for the conservation of lepidopterans worldwide. We primarily focus on doing historical reconstructions of past ecologies by using the wealth of data available in natural history museum collections and participatory science platforms.
