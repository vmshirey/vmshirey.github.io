How are arthropods responding to global climate change?

My research investigates this question in some of Earth's coldest ecosystems.

Learn More

Research Themes

Arthropods are the most biodiverse animals on the planet, yet so much is unknown about how they are responding to climate change. This knowledge gap is especially amplified towards the poles, where both sampling and study of terrestrial arthropods is sparse. My research aims to fill these knowledge gaps at multiple spatiotemporal scales through three main focus areas.

Biodiversity Informatics

Central to all of my work is an understanding of the data ecosystem. Since many of the groups that I work with are understudied, data from various resources needs to be pulled together and analyzed using appropriate methodologies.
