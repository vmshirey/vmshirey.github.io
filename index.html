About Me

I am a current Biology Ph.D. candidate in the Ries Lab of Butterfly Informatics at Georgetown University, located in Washington, D.C. My dissertation research, born from my interest in cold habitats, investigates the application of museum specimen and community science data for reconstructing how facets of historical ecologies have shifted with recent climate change. I'm also particularly interested in questions surrounding metacommunities; emergent patterns along the axis of morphology, behavior, and population dynamics; and applications of machine-learning and probabilistic modeling towards ecology.

I obtained my B.S. in Environmental Sciences from Drexel University (advised by Dr. Jon Gelhaus and Dr. Richard McCourt) and was a Fulbright Finland Research Fellow from 2017-2018 where I was hosted by Dr. Pedro Cardoso at the Finnish Museum of Natural History - University of Helsinki/Laboratory for Integrative Biodiversity Research.
