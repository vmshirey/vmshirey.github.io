About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Ries Lab of Butterfly Informatics within the Department of Biology at Georgetown University. My research, generally, focuses on discovering how facets of Earth's biodiversity are responding to environmental stressors, in particular, recent global change. I use a variety of tools to answer these questions and aid in scientific discovery including statistical inference (largely, hierarchical Bayesian models) and machine learning.

I obtained my B.S. in Environmental Sciences from Drexel University in Philadelphia where my undergraduate research focused on crane fly (Diptera: Tipulidae) systematics and biodiversity informatics. Before starting my graduate studies, I was a Fulbright Finland Research Fellow hosted by Dr. Pedro Cardoso at the Laboratory for Integrative Biodiversity Research at the Finnish Museum of Natural History - University of Helsinki.
