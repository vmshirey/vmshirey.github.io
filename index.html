As a biodiversity data scientist, I am broadly interested in developing and using new computational tools to reconstruct and predict how communities have responded/will respond to stressful environmental conditions. I work mostly with insects as a study system and am interested in questions surrounding community composition, color, and morphology.

A little more detail: I am currently a Ph.D. candidate and National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellow in the Ries Laboratory of Butterfly Informatics in the Department of Biology at Georgetown University. Prior to my doctoral studies, I was a Fulbright Finland Grantee to the Laboratory for Integrative Biodiversity Research at the Finnish Museum of Natural History - University of Helsinki. I obtained my B.S. in environmental science from Drexel University in Philadelphia.
