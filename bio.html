About Me

I completed my B.S. in Environmental Science with a focus on systematics and biodiversity at Drexel University, located in Philadelphia, in 2017. During that time, I also picked up a dual-minor in Bioinformatics and Sustainable Design. I worked extensively in the Entomology Collections at the Academy of Natural Sciences of Drexel University, the oldest natural history collection on the continent. At Drexel, I was advised by Dr. Jon Gelhaus, Dr. Richard McCourt, and Steven Dilliplane.

After graduating from Drexel in 2017, I relocated to Helsinki, Finland on a Fulbright Research Fellowship to Luonnontielteellinen Keskusmuseo (The Finnish Museum of Natural History) at the University of Helsinki. I worked under the mentorship of Dr. Pedro Cardoso on topics related to global spider conservation. In Finland I became fascinated with boreal forest and Arctic habitats and decided to make those regions the focus on my graduate research.

I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in Biology at Georgetown University, located in Washington, D.C. Dr. Leslie Ries in the department serves as my major advisor. My dissertation research focuses on reconstructing the historical ecologies and morphology of cold-adapted butterflies from sparse, unstructured data such as occurrences and photographs from natural history museums and community science programs. I employ a wide array of statistical and machine-learning approaches to these data sets.
