Reconciliation for a tiny wrestler

Digitally altered image.

Conflicts with identity often arise in my exploration of self, especially when those conflicts result from childhood trauma. Here my outline is still intact, referencing that I can never truly remove myself from past events that have shaped my responses to external stimuli; however, by reorganizing the tiniest elements that make up my body in this picture, I hope to reframe this experience away from the fear and anxiety present in the original image. Instead of focusing on the fear of causing harm, I choose to recognize that fear is egodystonic and actually references a strong personal orientation towards healing and reconciliation.

I created this work by applying a subject mask and iteratively sorting the pixel values within that mask by intensity value using Python code. I especially like how the exactness of the underlying logic used to create this image results in uncertainty.
