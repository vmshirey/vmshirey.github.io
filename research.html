Research Overview

My research interests lie at the intersection of biodiversity informatics and global change ecology. I consider myself largely agnostic when it comes to a study taxon, but prefer working with arthropods in general. Major themes in my work are highlighted below:

Biodiversity Informatics, Museums, and Modeling

Underpinning all of my work is a firm understanding of best practices and analytical approaches in handling unstructured, large data-sets.

Communities and Global Change

I apply heirarchical modeling techniques to reconstruct community composition and to better understand how communities are responding to global change processes.

Color, Pattern, and Climate Change

Using photography, I analyze how color and patterning are changing in organisms over long time periods.
