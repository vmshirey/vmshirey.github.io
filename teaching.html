Teaching Philosophy and Mentorship

I co-developed a community ecology lab for Georgetown's general ecology coursework that has since been integrated into the regular teaching schedule. This lab focuses on leaf litter arthropod communities and explores topics such as ecological community metrics as well as arthropod identification. I have also had the opportunity to independently write laboratory exercises for Georgetown's global climate change ecology course for upper-level bachelor-level and graduate students. In these labs, I focused on developing testable hypotheses for and the implementation of species distribution models for current and future species range projections.
