Vaughn M. Shirey

Perspective on Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

We all come to science with different backgrounds and identities; unforuntately, science has continued to exlude individuals with certain backgrounds from participating as their whole selves. As a queer, neurodivergent, and disabled scientist, I am intimately familiar with the cisheternormative and ableist aspects of science, especially biodiversity science, and how these systems exlude people like me from participating in it.
